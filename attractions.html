Famous Attractions

Discover the architectural marvels of ancient Rome

The Colosseum

Piazza del Colosseo, Rome Construction: 70-80 AD

The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, stands as the largest amphitheater ever built. This oval-shaped arena could hold between 50,000 and 80,000 spectators who came to watch gladiatorial contests, animal hunts, executions, re-enactments of famous battles, and dramas based on Classical mythology.

Despite being partially ruined due to damage caused by earthquakes and stone-robbers, the Colosseum remains an iconic symbol of Imperial Rome and one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world.

Interesting Facts

  • The Colosseum could be filled with water for mock naval battles
  • It took only 8 years to build this massive structure
  • The arena featured a complex underground system called the hypogeum

Visitor Information

Opening Hours: 8:30 AM - 7:00 PM (varies by season)

Admission: 16 Euros (combined ticket with Roman Forum)

Tip: Book tickets online to avoid long queues

The Pantheon

Piazza della Rotonda, Rome Construction: 126 AD

The Pantheon is a former Roman temple, now a church, on the site of an earlier temple commissioned by Marcus Agrippa during the reign of Augustus. The present building was completed by the emperor Hadrian and probably dedicated about 126 AD.

Its magnificent dome remains the world's largest unreinforced concrete dome, featuring a central opening (oculus) that allows natural light to illuminate the interior. The perfect proportions of the building—the height to the oculus equals the diameter of the dome—showcase Roman engineering at its finest.

Interesting Facts

  • The oculus is 30 feet in diameter and remains open to the elements
  • The building has been in continuous use throughout its history
  • The bronze doors are original, dating back to 126 AD

Visitor Information

Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 7:00 PM

Admission: Free

Tip: Visit early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds

Roman Forum

Via della Salara Vecchia, Rome Development: 8th century BC - 5th century AD

The Roman Forum (Forum Romanum) was the central area around which ancient Rome developed, where commercial, religious, economic, political, legal, and social activities occurred. Citizens of ancient Rome referred to this space as the Forum Magnum or simply the Forum.

Today, the Forum stands as a vast archaeological site, containing the ruins of several important ancient government buildings. Visitors can walk through centuries of Roman history, from the early Republic to the fall of the Empire.

Interesting Facts

  • The Forum was the site of many important Roman political speeches
  • It contains the Temple of Saturn, which housed the Roman treasury
  • The Via Sacra, the main street through the Forum, was part of Roman triumph processions

Visitor Information

Opening Hours: 8:30 AM - 7:00 PM (varies by season)

Admission: 16 Euros (combined ticket with Colosseum)

Tip: Wear comfortable shoes and bring water, as there is limited shade
